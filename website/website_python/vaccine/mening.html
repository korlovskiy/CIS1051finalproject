Meningococcal

About the virus:

Meningococcal disease refers to any illness caused by bacteria called Neisseria meningitidis.

These illnesses are often severe, can be deadly, and include infections of the lining of the brain and spinal cord (meningitis) and bloodstream.

Ways to contract the virus:

Generally, it takes close (for example, coughing or kissing) or lengthy contact to spread these bacteria

People spread meningococcal bacteria to other people by sharing respiratory and throat secretions (saliva or spit).

Viral symptoms:

sudden fever

headache

stiff neck

vomiting

cold hands and feet

rapid breathing

diarrhea

The later stages, a dark purple rash.

Additional Information:

Anyone can get meningococcal disease, but rates of disease are highest in children younger than 1 year old, followed by a second peak in adolescence.

Rates of meningococcal disease have declined in the United States since the 1990s and remain low today.
