Zoster recombinant (Shingles)

About the virus:

Shingles (also called herpes zoster, or just zoster) is a painful skin rash, that is caused by varicella-zoster virus, the same virus that causes chickenpox.

Ways to contract the virus:

People get shingles when the varicella zoster virus, which causes chickenpox, reactivates in their bodies after they have already had chickenpox. After a person recovers from chickenpox, the virus stays dormant (inactive) in the body. This virus can reactivate years later, causing shingles.

Viral symptoms:

Pain, itching, or tingling of the skin followed by a painful rash of blister-like sores, usually on one side of the body, often on the face or torso.

Fever

Headache

Chills

Upset stomach

Additional Information:

The risk of shingles increases with age.

About 1 out of every 3 people in the United States will develop shingles, also known as herpes zoster, in their lifetime.

An estimated 1 million people get shingles each year in this country.
