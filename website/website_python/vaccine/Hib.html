Haemophilus influenzae type b (Hib)

About the virus:

Hib is a bacterial illness that can lead to a potentially deadly brain infection in young children.

It may cause diseases such as meningitis (inflammation of the coverings of the brain and spinal column), bloodstream infections, pneumonia, arthritis and infections of other parts of the body.

Ways to contract the virus:

People spread H. influenzae, including Hib, to others through respiratory droplets.

Those who are infected spread the bacteria by coughing or sneezing, which creates small respiratory droplets that contain the bacteria.

Viral symptoms:

Fever and chills

Cough

Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing

Sweating

Bloodstream infections

Altered mental status (confusion)

Additional Information:

Between 3% to 6% of Hib cases in children are fatal.

Up to 20% of patients who survive Hib meningitis have permanent hearing loss or other long-term neurological sequelae.
