Varicella (chickenpox)

About the virus:

Acute infectious disease caused by varicella-zoster virus (VZV) that was distinguished from smallpox at the end of the 19th century.

Ways to contract the virus:

The virus spreads mainly through close contact with someone who has chickenpox to others who have never had the disease or never been vaccinated.

If one person has it, up to 90% of the people close to that person who are not immune will also become infected.

Viral symptoms:

fever

tiredness

loss of appetite

headache

Bacterial infections of the skin and soft tissues (severe rashes)

Infection of the lungs (pneumonia)

Infection or swelling of the brain

Bleeding problems

Bloodstream infections

Dehydration

Additional Information:

Some people with serious complications from chickenpox can become so sick that they need to be hospitalized. Chickenpox can also cause death.
